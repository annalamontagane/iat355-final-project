Visualizations

All eight exploratory visualizations from our data exploration phase. Each answers a specific analytical question relevant to fraud detection rule performance and missed-fraud patterns.

Visualization 01

Rule Financial Impact vs. Missed Fraud Count

Scatter plot mapping each of the 15 fraud detection rules by false negative count (x-axis) versus estimated financial impact in thousands of dollars (y-axis), colored by fix priority. Reveals which rules have the highest combined severity.

Scatter PlotPriority Color Encodingfraud_rules_rows.csvD3 · scaleLinear · circles
Key Insight

VD-338b and CA-221 form the high-impact cluster in the upper-right quadrant. Most High priority rules map cleanly to high FN count and high financial loss, confirming that priority assignments reflect real severity. The two IP/Geo rules show low FN counts but medium priority, suggesting they affect a niche but important transaction segment.

Visualization 02

Missed Fraud Trends by Region

Weekly rolling average of missed fraud counts over 180 days, broken down by four geographic regions (North America, EMEA, APAC, LATAM). North America consistently has the highest volume; all regions show periodic spikes modeled on seasonal fraud surges.

Multi-line Time SeriesRegional Breakdowndaily_aggregates_rows.csvD3 · scaleTime · curveCatmullRom
Key Insight

North America consistently logs the highest missed fraud counts, while LATAM remains the most stable. Periodic spikes across all regions validate the need for date-range filtering and region-level drill-down in the dashboard, aligning with the trend exploration task from our proposal.

Visualization 03

Transaction Amount Distribution by Fraud Outcome

Overlapping histogram showing the distribution of transaction amounts for Legitimate, Confirmed Fraud, and Pending Review transactions. Reveals the dramatic difference in amount profiles between fraudulent and legitimate activity.

Overlapping HistogramOutcome Comparisontransactions_rows.csvD3 · d3.bin · scaleBand
Key Insight

Legitimate transactions are heavily concentrated below $200. Fraudulent transactions spread broadly across $200–$3,000. This strong separation confirms that transaction amount is a discriminating feature, and validates including amount-based filtering when analysts drill down into flagged transactions.

Visualization 04

False Positives by Rule Category

Horizontal bar chart showing the number of legitimate transactions incorrectly flagged by each rule category. Device-based rules generate the most false positives — consistent with Android device fingerprinting limitations noted in stakeholder interviews.

Horizontal BarCategory Comparisontransactions_rows.csvD3 · scaleBand · scaleLinear
Key Insight

Device rules account for 212 false positives — nearly double the next category. This directly informs the dashboard design: analysts need to filter and sort rules by false positive impact to prioritize optimization. ML-Model rules also contribute notably, suggesting static thresholds need dynamic adjustment.

Visualization 05

Risk Score Distribution by Fraud Outcome

Box plots comparing the distribution of calculated risk scores across Legitimate, Pending Review, and Confirmed Fraud transactions. Highlights the grey zone at 30–55 where false positives and missed fraud most frequently occur.

Box PlotDistribution Comparisontransactions_rows.csvD3 · d3.quantile · scaleBand
Key Insight

Confirmed fraud centers around a risk score of ~72 while legitimate transactions center around ~25. The 30–55 grey zone is where most misclassifications occur. The dashboard should surface this ambiguous range for analyst review, since these are the transactions that most benefit from rule tuning.

Visualization 06

Fraud Rate by Transaction Country

Horizontal bar chart showing the fraud rate (percentage of confirmed fraud transactions) for the top 10 countries by fraud rate, filtered to countries with at least 30 transactions. The 15% threshold line marks elevated risk.

Horizontal BarGeographic Comparisontransactions_rows.csvD3 · scaleBand · % encoding
Key Insight

Russia, Nigeria, and India show the highest fraud rates (above 15%), while countries like the United States and Canada have lower rates despite much higher transaction volumes. This geographic disparity confirms the need for country-level filtering in the Deep Dive level of the dashboard.

Visualization 07

Daily False Positives vs. Missed Fraud

Scatter plot showing the daily relationship between total false positives and total missed fraud across all regions, with a computed linear trend line and correlation coefficient. Tests whether high-FP days are also high-missed-fraud days.

Scatter PlotCorrelation Analysisdaily_aggregates_rows.csvD3 · linear regression · scaleLinear
Key Insight

The weak positive correlation (r ~0.06) shows that false positives and missed fraud are largely independent on a day-to-day basis. Both problems require separate analytical attention, which is why the dashboard presents them as distinct KPIs rather than a combined metric.

Visualization 08

Distribution of Top Failure Types

Donut chart showing the relative frequency of the most common rule failure type recorded each day across all regions over the 180-day period. Reveals whether any single failure mode dominates analyst attention.

Donut ChartProportional Distributiondaily_aggregates_rows.csvD3 · d3.pie · d3.arc
